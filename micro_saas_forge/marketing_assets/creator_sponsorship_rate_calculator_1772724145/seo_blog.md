## The Big Problem With Standard Sponsorship Pricing
Most creators use the tired formula: `(Average views / 1000) * $15 CPM` to set their rates. This is broken for three core reasons:
1. **Niche value is ignored**: A B2B SaaS creator has a CPM 4x higher than a comedy skit creator, because B2B brands have far higher customer lifetime value and will pay a premium for targeted audiences.
2. **Engagement isn’t weighted**: A creator with a 6% engagement rate drives 3x more conversions than a creator with a 2% engagement rate in the same niche, but generic CPM calculators treat them the same.
3. **Content type is excluded**: A dedicated full-length video where you test a product for 8 minutes delivers far more ROI for a brand than a 2-second shoutout, but standard calculators don’t add multipliers for high-intent content.
For example, a 100k subscriber home renovation creator with 4% engagement would only get a $2,000 rate estimate from a generic CPM calculator. The actual market rate for a dedicated video for a power tool brand is closer to $8,000—meaning they’re leaving $6,000 on the table per deal.

## How to Calculate Data-Backed Sponsorship Rates (Manual Formula)
Before we introduce the tool that automates this entire process, here’s the 4-step formula we built our calculator on, so you understand the logic:
1. **Start with your niche base CPM**: Use 2024 average niche CPMs:
   - Finance, B2B SaaS: $35-$60
   - Home improvement, parenting: $25-$32
   - Lifestyle, beauty: $20-$28
   - Gaming, comedy: $12-$18
2. **Adjust for engagement**: Multiply your base CPM by `(your engagement rate / average niche engagement rate)`. Most niches have a 2% average engagement rate, so if you have 4% engagement, double your base CPM.
3. **Add content type multiplier**:
   - Dedicated video/post: 1.8x
   - Integrated mention: 1.2x
   - Short-form video (Reel/Short/TikTok): 0.6x
   - Newsletter feature: 1x
   - Blog post: 0.9x
4. **Calculate final rate**: `(Average views per content piece / 1000) * adjusted CPM`
