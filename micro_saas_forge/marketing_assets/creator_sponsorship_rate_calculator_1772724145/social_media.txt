=== REDDIT POST ===
**I built a sponsorship rate calculator for creators because I was tired of guessing.**

I’m a developer who also runs a small YouTube channel, and I kept hitting the same wall: trying to figure out what to charge for a sponsored segment. Most advice out there is either “charge $X per 1,000 views” or totally anecdotal. That’s fine for a starting point, but it ignores everything that actually matters—like niche, engagement rate, and how the sponsorship is delivered (e.g., a dedicated video vs. an integrated mention).

So I made a dynamic calculator that actually uses those variables to generate a credible, data-backed rate range. It’s not just a static CPM multiplier; it adjusts based on:
- **Niche** (tech, gaming, lifestyle, etc.)
- **Engagement rate** (comments, likes relative to views/subscribers)
- **Content type** (dedicated video, integrated segment, or simple mention)
- **Platform** (YouTube or written content/blog)

The logic pulls from aggregated industry rate data, applies modifiers for niche “heat” and engagement quality, and spits out a range that feels realistic rather than random. I built it with Next.js 14 (App Router), TypeScript, and Tailwind, hosted on Vercel. The state logic for the interactive sliders and selectors got a little gnarly, but it was fun to tune.

If you create content or know someone who does, I’d love for you to try it and tear it apart:

**https://creator-sponsorship-rate-calculator-ihyfzwaj9.vercel.app**

This started as a tool for myself, but I’m hoping it can help other creators avoid undercharging or overpricing themselves. Would really appreciate brutal feedback—especially if the calculations feel off for your niche, or if there are other factors I should bake in.
