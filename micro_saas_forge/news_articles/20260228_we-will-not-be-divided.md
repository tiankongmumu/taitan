A powerful statement is making the rounds on HackerNews, striking a chord with over 500 upvotes. It’s not a new framework or a groundbreaking library—it’s a declaration. The site **[notdivided.org](https://notdivided.org)** hosts a simple, potent manifesto titled "We Will Not Be Divided." In an industry often fragmented by tech stack wars, licensing battles, and toxic debates, this initiative calls for a return to core, unifying principles in software development.

So, what is it? The manifesto outlines a set of commitments for builders. It advocates for tools that are open and interoperable, for communities that prioritize constructive collaboration over criticism, and for technology that serves humanity rather than entrenches division. It’s a conscious pushback against the silos and friction that can slow down innovation and demoralize developers. In essence, it’s a reminder that we’re all here to build, and that common ground is our most powerful asset.

Why does this matter now? The **tech community** is at an inflection point. The pace of change is exhilarating, but it can also be alienating. New developers face a dizzying array of conflicting opinions, while veterans can grow weary of cyclical arguments. This manifesto matters because it focuses on the *how* and the *why* behind the code. It champions **open source ethics** not just as a licensing model, but as a philosophy of transparency and shared progress. By agreeing on foundational **software principles** like respect, pragmatism, and inclusivity, we can channel our collective energy into solving real problems.

Who should care? Anyone who builds, maintains, or depends on software. This is especially crucial for open-source maintainers drowning in issue reports, team leads navigating internal tech debates, and community managers fostering healthy spaces. It’s a guiding star for **collaborative development** at any scale. If you’ve ever felt frustrated by unproductive conflict or the "not invented here" syndrome, this manifesto offers a framework for a better way forward.

In a landscape often defined by what separates us, "We Will Not Be Divided" is a timely plea for focus on what unites us: the desire to create meaningful technology, together.
