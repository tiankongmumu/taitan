What if you could clone Garry Tan's exact AI-powered development setup? That's the promise of **gstack**, a new, opinionated collection of six tools that's taking GitHub by storm. Created by the renowned investor and former Posthaven co-founder, this isn't just another list of cool apps. It's a curated, battle-tested system designed to act as your personal CEO, Engineering Manager, Release Manager, and QA Engineer—all powered by Anthropic's Claude Code.

At its core, gstack is a **productivity** blueprint. It outlines a specific, integrated workflow using tools like Cursor (an AI-native IDE), Windsurf, and GitHub Copilot, configured to work in concert. The "opinionated" part is key: Tan has pre-defined how these tools should interact, removing the paralysis of choice and configuration hell that often bogs down developers. This setup automates everything from high-level project strategy and code review to writing tests and managing releases, effectively creating a seamless **automation** pipeline for the entire software development lifecycle.

So why does this matter for the average developer or small team? In an era of overwhelming tool sprawl, a coherent, pre-vetted stack is incredibly valuable. It shortcuts months of experimentation and provides a proven framework for leveraging AI to its fullest potential. For solo founders, indie hackers, or small engineering teams without dedicated management roles, gstack offers a force multiplier. It systematizes best practices, ensures consistency, and lets you focus on building rather than managing the process of building.

Who should care? Primarily **developer**s and technical founders looking to maximize their output with AI. If you're already using Claude or Copilot in a fragmented way and want a more powerful, integrated experience, gstack is your playbook. It's also a fascinating case study for anyone interested in the future of AI-augmented **workflow**s, demonstrating how large language models can be orchestrated to handle distinct, high-level roles within a project.
