**Body:**
Hey everyone,

Like many of you, I've been following the discussion about Meta's AI glasses and the insane amount of data they (and basically all our devices) collect. The HackerNews thread was a real eye-opener.

It got me thinking about my own photo library. We share photos all the time, thinking we're just sharing an image. But they're packed with EXIF data, sometimes GPS coordinates, camera fingerprints, and now even AI generation markers.

I'm a developer, so I teamed up with a friend and we built a simple web tool to address this specific panic. It's called **AI Privacy Scanner**.

You just drag and drop a photo. It instantly analyzes it and shows you a breakdown of what's hidden inside: GPS location (if present), device model, software tags, potential AI markers, and gives it a privacy risk score. Then, with one click, you can strip all that metadata and download a clean, safe copy.

We're not a company. We're just privacy nerds who made this over a weekend. It's completely free, no sign-up, and runs in your browser (client-side processing for the stripping part).

I'd love for you to try it and give honest feedback. Does it miss something? Is the risk score helpful? The goal is to make people aware of what they're actually sharing.
